## Design Context

Working baseline inferred from the current ALPS conference site until the project team refines it further.

### Users
Primary visitors are prospective ALPS conference attendees: researchers, clinicians, therapists, students, and curious professionals deciding whether to attend. They need fast confidence in the event's credibility, practical logistics, and overall quality of the in-person experience.

### Brand Personality
Science-forward, elevated, welcoming. The experience should feel trustworthy and cultured first, then atmospheric and memorable. The emotional goals are confidence, curiosity, and anticipation.

### Aesthetic Direction
Preserve the site's dark, cinematic conference aesthetic with restrained blue and pink accents, crisp typography, and subtle motion. Use real imagery and venue details to ground the experience, and avoid generic SaaS layouts or loud psychedelic cliches.

### Design Principles
- Lead with credibility before spectacle.
- Use atmosphere as an accent, not as a substitute for clarity.
- Favor real-world cues such as venue photography, dates, and logistics over decorative filler.
- Keep layouts composed and readable across desktop and mobile.
- Maintain a premium academic-event tone: modern, calm, and intentional.
